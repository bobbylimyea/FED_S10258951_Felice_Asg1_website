Exercise

What is exercise?

Exercise is the physical activity that is done to maintain or improve health and fitness. Exercise is also vital to maintain our physical and mental health.

Why is exercise important?

Exercise is important as exercise helps to train the body to improve its function and enhance its fitness. Doing physical activities regularly is necessary for our health because it helps to increase our chances of living a longer and healthier life. Furthermore, exercising helps to make the bones, muscles, and joints healthier and helps to make us feel better about ourselves.

Benefits of exercise (Healthline, 2023).

1. Exercise makes you happier

  • Exercise helps to boost serotonin and norepinephrine which helps to relieve depression. Furthermore, exercise also helps to increase endorphins, which helps us to feel more positive.

2. Exercise can help to reduce the risks of chronic disease

It can help to reduce diseases such as:

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Heart disease
  • Cancer [such as lung and liver cancer]
  • High LDL cholesterol [low-density lipoprotein]
  • Hypertension

3. Exercise can help with your brain health and memory

  • Exercise increases your heart rate. This promotes the flow of blood and oxygen to your brain. It can also stimulate the production of hormones that enhance the growth of brain cells. Additionally, exercise helps to reduce changes in the brain that can contribute to conditions like Alzheimers disease and dementia.

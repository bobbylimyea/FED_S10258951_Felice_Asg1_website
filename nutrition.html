Nutrition

What is Nutrition?

Nutrition is a crucial part of our health and development. Having a regular, balanced diet keeps the mechanisms in our body working.

There are 6 major groups of nutrients we need to function:

(Center for Health Protection, n.d.)

  • Carbohydrates üçû
    • Carbohydrates are our main source of energy. It is mainly found in grains, but are also found in fruit, root vegetables, and dairy products
  • Proteins üí™
    • Builds and repairs muscles and bones and to make hormones and enzymes and are found in meat, seafood, eggs, dairy products, and beans
  • Fats
    • Fat protects organs, and prevents us from losing heat in extreme cold weather. They also transport fat-soluble vitamins such as vitamin A, D, E and K. Found in meat, dairy products, nuts, seeds and edible oils.
  • Vitamins and Minerals
    • They are micronutrients. There are a variety of them such as Vitamin D, C, calcium, and iron. They work together to keep our body working.
  • Dietary Fibre
    • Dietary fibre helps with digestion and are found in wholegrain cereals, and fruit and vegetables
  • Water ü´ó
    • Regulates our body temperature, produces bodily fluids, transports nutrients and removes waste products.

Why it's important

  • Eating healthy protects you from chronic noncommunicable diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, stroke, cancer, and osteoporosis.

  • It provides us with energy to function throughout the day.

  • Improves our well-being

  • Improves our ability to recover from illness or injury
